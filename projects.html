Hotter or colder Grace Copplestone

An alternative weather forecast | link | code

The app

Hotter or colder quite simply makes a comparison of the weather. GIven that the brain relies so heavily on comparisons, it seemed sensible to create a more intuitive weather forecast. Users can also submit a new location to be added to the site. Future developments might include additional comments. e.g. Rather than wetter or drier - Whether you should or shouldn't take an umbrella to work tomorrow.

The tech

The project was made using Sinatra and is hosted by Heroku. The hardest bit initially was fetching the data from the MET Office and breaking down the huge hashes to get the numbers I needed. I'm storing the information in a database using MongoDB. That was pretty tricky but it saved me later on when I wanted to add the additional buttons as I was just able to pull out the extra numbers I had already stored. Hotter or colder is not currently a profitable product however I imagine it's of a similar standard to that needed for an MVP. Therefore, on top of the coding I've gained a vague idea of the time required to get through the first cycle of the Lean Startup.

Language Love Letters Sally Poundall

Send personalized love messages in the language of your choice. | link | code

The app

My app allows people to send personalized love messages in the native language of their partner. I actually came up with the idea when I came across this quote from Nelson Mandela 'If you talk to a man in a language he understands, it goes to his head. If you talk to him in his own language, it goes to his heart.' From my experience this quote is so very true and so I thought it would be a nice idea to set up an app that allowed English people to send love messages to their boyfriends/girlfriends/spouses in their native language. Also this app was the perfect project for me because it combined my love of languages with my new-found interest in coding :)

The tech

My app uses:

- ruby

- sinatra

- a teensy bit of mongoid, which allows me to display the number of love messages that have been sent

- a little bit of jquery which makes the social media buttons change colour on hover

Other things I learnt:

- how to add a custom email to google (mail@languageloveletters.com), and then make this email the default from which all messages are sent from

- how to add a custom domain to my heroku app (www.languageloveletters.com)

- how to create forms with bootstrap (took me a while to figure them out!)

- how to create my own social media buttons that sit with the look and feel of my webpage, and which link to twitter, google+ and facebook

- how to create images with transparent backgrounds using paint.net - I learnt this through adding the social media buttons

- a smattering of Spanish & German (though I can't say I've come away with any Russian haha)

To be honest the whole thing was hard; trying to get those social media buttons to look good and to change colour when I wanted was a mission in itself; also working out how to set out the structure of the website (what should come first? entering details or choosing a language?) was a bit of a headache, trying to get all information in the right place so that when it comes to sending the email all the right information is there. Also trying to find the love messages themselves was very time consuming, though I guess you wouldn't call that 'difficult'. The easiest bit for me was doing the CSS and making it all look pretty :) All in all I learnt a hell of a lot doing this project and I can safely say I've come away from CodeFirst:Girls with something I'm really proud of!

The app

This allows the Expedition Society to add new trips, allowing members of the society to see which trips are available to sign up for, as well as view other trips that have happened.

Future Expansion: Have a sign up button where the user can fill out a mini form to sign up to a trip, and then they get an e-mail, saying if they are on the trip or on the reserve list.

Have a log in system, where by only committee members can add and edit trips.

Most of the learning was done through de-bugging, which made bits and bobs of my knowledge come together, to understand the whole system.

Sclogs Nadia Odunayo

Discovering Super-Curriculars | link | code

Username: sclog@example.com; Password: sclogtest

The app

My project represents an online space for 12-15 year olds to discover super-curricular activities and keep a record of ones that they take part in. The aim is to introduce young secondary school students to the idea of going beyond the school syllabus to learn new things and develop new interests that will serve them well when applying to university and beyond.

By keeping track of their super-curricular activities, students can refer back to ideas they've discovered, develop them, and share with their friends and family.

The tech

The project is based in Rails. I began by setting up a static page framework in Rails, before incorporating a blog controller. Hence, the first thing I had to learn was how to work with the two different frameworks and how to integrate them.

After putting up some initial content, I then set up a user login system using Devise. This was pretty straightforward. The most difficult part was working out how to make Sclog posts private, such that users could only see their posts when they logged in and not everybody else's. I also had to discover how to put in some security measures to prevent users from discovering other Sclog posts that they haven't authored.
